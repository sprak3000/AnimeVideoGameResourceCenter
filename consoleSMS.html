Sega Master System

In 1986, Sega decided to challenge Nintendo's growing hold on the video game industry. The Sega Master System was released and was considered technically superior to the NES. Despite being technically superior in hardware, the SMS suffered when it came to software. Nintendo's earlier release date to a video-game starved market and their policy of having third-party developers only develop for the NES prevented the SMS from achieving a large user base.

That does not mean that great software wasn't developed for the SMS. One of the most respected RPG games ever was Phantasy Star. This SMS game was an instant classic and spawned many sequels. In 1990, Sega released the Sega Master System II. This was a scaled-down version of the SMS aimed at gathering a larger user base through inexpensive hardware. Those that did not want to buy the Sega Genesis could now buy the previous system at a cheap price.

Sega did not implement a territorial lockout on the SMS. All Japanese and European games could be played on the system. Master System games could also be played on the Genesis through the Power Base Convertor. By using the Master Gear convertor, you could play some of the Master System games on your Game Gear as well.
