Sega Genesis

Sega scored a big hit against Nintendo in 1989 by releasing the Sega Genesis. The Genesis was the second 16-bit game system to appear (the PC-Engine in Japan being the first). The Genesis was known as the Mega Drive in Japan. The Genesis did not make a large dent in the Japanese market, but the US market made it a solid contender against Nintendo.

The language lock-out on the Genesis involves a built-in language switch and megahertz settings in the Megadrive (the Japanese version of the Genesis). Since the Genesis has neither of these, your only hope is to build a language switch and cut plastic from your Genesis to make the cartrige fit or buy a converter with a language switch. Many Genesis games contain the roms for their Japanese counterparts, such as Mystic Defender (Kujaku-Ou). If a language switch is set to Japanese, it plays the original game. -from Doug Cranston
