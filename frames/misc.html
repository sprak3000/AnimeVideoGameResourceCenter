Odds and Ends

As people have sent me information, a lot of it has ended up in the bit bucket. Well, I have decided to recycle some of this information on this page. What you will find in here is various bits of trivia as well as miscellaneous FAQs that just don't fit in anywhere else at this time. Enjoy!
Did you know that Spellcaster for the Master System and Mystic Defender for the Genesis are actually retouched Kujaku-Ou games? In case you don't know, Kujaku-Ou (Peacock King) is an occult/horror manga/anime series about a demon-killing warrior priest. -- Doug Cranston
Hey! Where are the anime games for the Nintendo 64! Well, there is one out right now. It's called Doraemon 64; it's a game much like Mario 64. Well, someone has already written a FAQ for it. Check it out!
Man! There are a lot of Goemon games on these pages. Well, according to Doug Cranston, Konami modeled the game character on Goemon's representations in Kabuki theater (like his afro).
Remember laser disc based arcade games like Dragon's Lair? Well, as some people may know, Cliffhanger used footage from Castle of Cagliostro and Mystery of Mamo to the same effect. Doug Cranston has also pointed out that Escape from Cyber City for the CDI uses footage from the two Galaxy Express movies as did the old and obscure arcade game Galaxy 999. Bega's Battle by Data East used footage from Harmageddon.
